Electric Vs Internal Combustion Engines

@Andrew Dao May 10, 2018

For people who don't know the fundamental difference between the two conventional, thermal cars(ICE) and electric cars is the process of transforming the potential energy into kinetic energy.( Better review your elementary notes now, basically fancy names for stored energy vs movement ) In internal combustion engined powered cars this energy is stored in a chemical form and is released through a chemical reaction inside the engine( mixing of air and fuel, with a spark ). Once again, a law we learned in elementary school, the conservation of matter says that in chemical reactions, the total mass of the product must equal the total mass of the reactants. This law is a very important concept I will touch on later, but basically whatever we are burning will have to get released back into the the environment somehow.( by gas form in this case) On the other hand, electric cars work differently, It stores chemical energy and releases the energy electrochemically, this means that there is no fuel being burned(chemical reaction) therefore no air pollutants through C02 while driving.

Why go electric?

@Andrew Dao May 10, 2018

People who support electric cars argue that EVs can produce zero tailpipe emissions, even when using fossil fuels to charge EVs contribute fewer emissions than ICE cars, ICE vehicles pollute continuously, and EV manufacturers use eco friendly materials.Any manufacturer can start using eco friendly materials when making a car so that point is null. Although they do not emit C02 while being driven, they do it in 3 other stages: during manufacturing, energy production , and at the end of the life cycle. The mining activities needed to extract the rare earth metals used in the batteries are very energy consuming and polluting. If the energy production is being powered with energy from burning fossil fuels, it is still releasing c02 in the power plant. When it comes to dealing with the batteries being recycled it is still expensive and most batteries are not being recycled.
